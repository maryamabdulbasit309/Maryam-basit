# Why I Created My GitHub Portfolio

As a Computer Engineering student, I am constantly learning new concepts, completing assignments, and working on different academic projects. Throughout this journey, I realized that much of my work remained scattered across notebooks, files, and university portals. Because of this, I wanted a central place where I could organize and present my learning experiences.

Creating a GitHub portfolio provided me with an opportunity to document my academic progress and showcase the skills I develop over time. Instead of treating projects and assignments as temporary tasks, I can now preserve them as part of a growing collection of work that reflects my learning journey.

Another reason for building this portfolio is to improve my technical communication skills. Understanding a concept is important, but being able to explain it clearly is equally valuable. By writing blog posts about topics I study, I can strengthen my understanding while sharing knowledge with others who may be learning the same subjects.

This portfolio also serves as a personal record of growth. Looking back at previous projects and posts will allow me to see how my skills evolve over time. Every project, assignment, and article represents a step forward in my development as an engineering student.

In the future, I plan to use this platform to share programming projects, digital logic design work, academic experiences, and insights gained throughout my Computer Engineering degree. My goal is to make this portfolio a meaningful representation of both my learning process and my professional growth.
