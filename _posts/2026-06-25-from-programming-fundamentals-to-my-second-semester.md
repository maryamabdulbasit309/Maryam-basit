# From Programming Fundamentals to My Second Semester

In my previous post, I shared why I decided to create this GitHub portfolio and how I wanted to document my learning journey as a Computer Engineering student. As I continue writing about my academic experiences, I feel it is important to go back to the point where many of my technical skills started developing. For me, that point was my first semester at UET Lahore, Faisalabad Campus, where I was introduced to Programming Fundamentals.

When I entered university, I was excited about studying Computer Engineering, but at the same time, I was unsure about what to expect. Like many students, I had heard about programming before joining university, but I had never written code seriously. During my first semester, Programming Fundamentals became one of the most important subjects in my academic life. It was not just another course that I had to pass. Instead, it became the foundation of how I learned to solve problems and think logically.

Programming Fundamentals was taught by Dr. Bilal Ahmad, who is a professor at UET Lahore, Faisalabad Campus. What made his teaching approach different was his focus on understanding concepts instead of memorizing solutions. From the very beginning, he encouraged students to think deeply about problems and develop logical approaches before writing any code. At first, I found programming difficult because every topic seemed completely new. Variables, data types, conditional statements, loops, and functions were concepts I had never studied before.

I still remember the confusion I felt when writing my first programs. Even a small mistake could prevent the entire program from working correctly. Sometimes I would spend a long time trying to identify a simple error. Although these situations were frustrating, they taught me patience and persistence. Over time, I realized that making mistakes was a natural part of learning programming. Every error helped me understand the language better and improved my problem-solving skills.

One thing I appreciated about Programming Fundamentals was that it trained us to think in a structured way. Instead of immediately looking for answers, I learned to break problems into smaller steps. This approach not only helped me in programming but also influenced how I handled challenges in other subjects. Gradually, I began viewing problems as opportunities to apply logical thinking rather than obstacles to avoid.

Another interesting aspect of learning under Dr. Bilal Ahmad was hearing about the broader applications of computing technologies. Apart from teaching Programming Fundamentals and Database Systems, he is also known for his expertise in Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, and Deep Learning. During different discussions, he often shared examples of how intelligent systems are developed to solve real-world problems. I found these discussions particularly inspiring because they showed us that programming is much more than writing code. It is a tool that can be used to create solutions with real impact.

One example that stayed with me was his emphasis on medical datasets. Dr. Bilal Ahmad often explained that medical data is usually collected with a high degree of precision and accuracy, making it valuable for training machine learning models. Hearing these examples helped me understand how the concepts we learn in the classroom can eventually contribute to solving meaningful problems in society. It also motivated me to take my studies more seriously because I could see the practical value of the skills I was developing.

As the first semester progressed, I started becoming more comfortable with programming concepts. Tasks that once seemed impossible gradually became manageable. I learned how to write programs using conditions, loops, and functions. More importantly, I learned how to think like a programmer. Instead of focusing only on writing code, I began focusing on understanding the logic behind a solution. This shift in mindset was one of the most valuable outcomes of the course.

By the time the first semester ended, I noticed a significant improvement in my confidence. I was no longer intimidated by programming problems. While I still had a lot to learn, I felt better prepared to face new academic challenges. The skills I developed during Programming Fundamentals became a strong foundation for the subjects I would study later.

When my second semester began, I entered it with a completely different mindset. I was already familiar with university life, assignments, quizzes, and academic expectations. More importantly, I had developed problem-solving habits that helped me approach new subjects with greater confidence. Although the second semester introduced new challenges, I felt more prepared than I had during my first days at university.

Looking back, I believe Programming Fundamentals was one of the most influential courses of my first semester. It taught me much more than programming syntax. It taught me logical thinking, patience, consistency, and the importance of continuous practice. These lessons continued to help me throughout my second semester and remain valuable even today.

As I continue documenting my journey through this portfolio, I realize that every semester builds upon the lessons of the previous one. The confidence and skills I gained from Programming Fundamentals became the foundation of my second semester experiences. In the coming posts, I will share how those skills helped me face new subjects, manage academic challenges, and continue growing as a Computer Engineering student at UET Lahore, Faisalabad Campus.
