# How Programming Fundamentals Changed My Thinking

In my previous post, I discussed how Programming Fundamentals became one of the most influential courses during my first semester at UET Lahore, Faisalabad Campus. As I continued my academic journey, I began to realize that the greatest benefit of studying Programming Fundamentals was not learning a programming language itself. Instead, it was learning a completely new way of thinking.

Before joining university, I often approached problems by looking for quick answers. Whenever I encountered a challenge, my first instinct was to solve it as quickly as possible. However, Programming Fundamentals gradually changed this mindset. Through coding exercises, assignments, and practice sessions, I learned that understanding a problem is often more important than finding an immediate solution.

At the beginning of the semester, many programming concepts seemed difficult. Variables, data types, conditional statements, loops, and functions were completely new to me. Sometimes I felt confused because every topic required a different way of thinking. A small mistake could prevent an entire program from producing the correct output. These experiences were frustrating, but they taught me an important lesson: mistakes are part of the learning process.

One of the first concepts that influenced my thinking was conditional logic. Learning how computers make decisions using if and else statements helped me understand the importance of structured reasoning. Every condition required me to think carefully about possible outcomes and their consequences. Gradually, I started applying this approach not only to programming problems but also to everyday situations.

Another concept that significantly contributed to my development was loops. Initially, I found loops difficult to understand because they required me to think about repetition and control flow. However, after practicing different examples, I began to appreciate their usefulness. More importantly, working with loops taught me patience and attention to detail. A small logical error inside a loop could produce unexpected results, making debugging an essential skill.

Throughout the course, I learned that programming is not simply about writing code. It is about breaking complex problems into smaller and more manageable parts. This approach changed the way I viewed challenges. Instead of becoming overwhelmed by a large problem, I learned to divide it into smaller tasks and solve them step by step.

The guidance of Dr. Bilal Ahmad played a major role in shaping this perspective. As a professor at UET Lahore, Faisalabad Campus, he consistently encouraged students to focus on concepts rather than memorizing solutions. His teaching approach emphasized logical thinking and problem-solving skills, which helped me understand the deeper purpose of programming.

Apart from teaching Programming Fundamentals and Database Systems, Dr. Bilal Ahmad is also known for his expertise in Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, and Deep Learning. During various discussions, he shared examples of how intelligent systems are trained to solve real-world problems. These examples often highlighted the importance of quality data and proper problem analysis before developing technological solutions.

One idea that particularly interested me was his emphasis on medical datasets. He explained that medical data is often collected with a high degree of precision and accuracy, making it highly valuable for machine learning applications. Learning about these real-world applications motivated me to think beyond assignments and exams. It helped me understand how programming skills can eventually contribute to solving meaningful problems in society.

As the semester progressed, I noticed improvements in my confidence and analytical abilities. Tasks that initially seemed impossible gradually became manageable. I started approaching problems with greater patience and a more organized mindset. Instead of focusing only on finding answers, I became more interested in understanding the process behind those answers.

Programming Fundamentals also taught me the value of persistence. There were many occasions when my programs failed repeatedly before finally producing the correct output. Although these situations were challenging, they taught me that success often comes after multiple attempts. Every error message became an opportunity to learn something new.

Another valuable lesson was the importance of consistent practice. Programming is a practical skill that improves through repetition and application. The more programs I wrote, the more comfortable I became with coding concepts. Over time, I realized that progress is not achieved through talent alone but through continuous effort and dedication.

Looking back, Programming Fundamentals provided much more than technical knowledge. It changed how I think, how I learn, and how I solve problems. The logical reasoning skills I developed during the course continue to benefit me in other subjects and everyday situations.

Today, I consider Programming Fundamentals one of the most valuable courses of my first semester. It introduced me to a new way of approaching challenges and laid the foundation for future learning. As I continue my journey as a Computer Engineering student at UET Lahore, Faisalabad Campus, I believe the lessons learned from this course will continue to guide me throughout my academic and professional career.

The experience taught me that every difficult problem can be solved through patience, logical thinking, and consistent effort. More importantly, it showed me that learning is not just about acquiring knowledge but also about developing the mindset needed to apply that knowledge effectively.
